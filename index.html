My latest work focuses on design systems

I have worked at small-to-medium businesses and large enterprises. No matter the size, all software companies have ambitious goals that come with the need for flexibility. Especially with the future unknowns in a product's life cycle.

By working in design systems, I help companies establish and maintain practices that promote software designers and developers working together as a unit.

Design systems provide infrastructure for scaling products efficiently and without technical and user experience debt. Systems infrastructure includes tangible frameworks and people processes. For example, a tangible framework could be a collection of well-documented design patterns, while a people process could be an established process behind UI contributions to a component library.

Systems infrastructure should be sustained and maintained by dedicated systems individuals. For a design system to succeed, consider investing in a design systems team or a decentralized model for maintaining design infrastructure.
